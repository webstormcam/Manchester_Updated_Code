COMING SOON

THE STORY

In the decades leading up to the Revolution and the Civil War, a phenomenal amount of building activity arose in Lexington. By the 1970s, the area became a destination for lavish Derby soirées thrown by local socialites. We designed our House to reflect the opulence of this time with an extravagant exterior shell that nods to the Antebellum architecture characterized by Georgian Revival mansions of the time, boudoir-style furnishings, fringe costume-inspired detailing, and the rich colors and textures of the Appalachian landscape.

GUEST ROOMS

Welcome to your pied-à-terre in Lexington, where you’ll find Georgian-revival architectural details, glamorous atelier-style furnishings, subtle equestrian leatherwork, and eclectic artwork reflecting the history of the region. OUR SPACES

GRANDDAM

Rooted in local Appalachian cuisine, our first-floor restaurant and bar serves elevated takes on well-known and well-loved regional dishes. Table-side preparations and interactive dishes create a casual, communal, and truly Southern experience in a rich and welcoming space.

LOST PALM

Vibrant juice-infused libations meet Southern comfort cuisine in this luxe and tropical oasis bar located on the rooftop of our 7th-floor. With a stage for live performances and space for popular backyard lawn games, Lost Palms also doubles as a playful, multi-activity lounge space.

OUR LOCATION

The Manchester is situated in the center of Lexington, Kentucky, a city rich with Southern charm and hospitality and marked by its strong heritage of horses, bourbon, and Bluegrass regional history. Known as the "Horse Capital of the World," and nicknamed the “Athens of the West,” Lexington is filled with local bars, distilleries, museums, parks, a thriving and diverse arts and culture scene, and a plethora of equine and Derby activities.
